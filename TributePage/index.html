SUMMARY

Yuan Longping (Chinese: 袁隆平; pinyin: Yuán Lóngpíng; September 7, 1930 – May 22, 2021) was a Chinese agronomist and inventor. He was a member of the Chinese Academy of Engineering known for developing the first hybrid rice varieties in the 1970s, part of the Green Revolution in agriculture.[1] For his contributions, Yuan is known as the "Father of Hybrid Rice".[2][3] Hybrid rice has since been grown in dozens of countries in Africa, America, and Asia—boosting food security and providing a robust food source in areas with a high risk of famine. The technology allowed China to sustain 20% of the global population on 9% of global arable land, an achievement in food security where he was awarded the 2004 World Food Prize and the 2004 Wolf Prize in Agriculture respectively.

TIMELINE

  • 1930 - Yuan Longping was born on September 7th, 1930 at Peking Union Medical College Hospital in Beijing, China.
  • 1953 - He graduated from Southwest Agricultural College (now part of Southwest University) in 1953.
  • 1959 - China experienced the Great Chinese Famine. Province.
  • 1961-1964 - Yuan discovered naturally-mutated male-sterile rice plants, laying the foundation for hybrid rice development.
  • 1964 - Yuan married Deng Ze, one of his students.
  • 1966 - Despite being named as a counter-revolutionary during the Cultural Revolution, Yuan received support from Nie Rongzhen, allowing him to continue his research.
  • 1970s - Yuan and his team found important wild rice varieties, leading to significant improvements in hybrid rice yields.
  • 1979 - Technique for hybrid rice introduced into the United States, marking the first case of intellectual property rights transfer in the history of the People's Republic of China.
  • 1980 - Yuan and his team donated crucial rice strains to the International Rice Research Institute, aiding global food supply chains.
  • 1999 - Record yield of 17,055 kilograms per hectare was registered for "Super Rice" in Yongsheng County, Yunnan Province.
  • 2014 - Yuan expressed support for genetically modified food as the future direction of food production.
  • 2021 - Yuan collapsed at his hybrid rice research base in Sanya on March 10. He was transferred to Changsha, Hunan Province for treatment on April 7. Yuan Longping died of multiple organ failure at Xiangya Hospital of Central South University at the age of 90.

Throughout his life, Yuan Longping made significant contributions to agriculture, especially through the development of hybrid rice varieties, earning him the title "Father of Hybrid Rice" and numerous honors and awards.
